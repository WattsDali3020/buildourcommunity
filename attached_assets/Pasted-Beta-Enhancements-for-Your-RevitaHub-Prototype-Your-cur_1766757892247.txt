Beta Enhancements for Your RevitaHub Prototype
Your current site is a strong start—clean, professional layout with the litepaper content effectively presented as a scrolling page. The title, abstract, problem stats, barriers, and solution cards display well, with good use of icons and formatting. To elevate it to a functional beta prototype while enabling user notifications for updates/iterations, focus on these additions and changes:

Add a Prominent Beta Banner: Place a fixed top banner saying "Beta Prototype – Join the Waitlist for Updates & Early Access" to set expectations and drive sign-ups.
Implement Waitlist/Notification Signup: Add a simple email capture form (e.g., via Replit + free tools like Mailchimp embed or Google Forms) with fields for email and optional interests (e.g., "Property Owner" or "Investor"). Promise "Notifications on new iterations, pilots, and launches."
Include Litepaper Download CTA: Add a button "Download Full Litepaper PDF" linking to your hosted file, with a note "Stay updated on progress."
Expand Navigation/Footer: Create a basic menu (Home, About, Roadmap, Waitlist, Contact) and footer with social links, disclaimer ("Beta: Functionality in development"), and your LLC details.
Create a Dedicated X Account: Yes—set up @RevitaHub or @BuildOurCommunityX for the company. Post prototype launch, share updates, and link back to the site. Use it to announce iterations and build community (e.g., "Follow for beta notifications").
Add Interactive Teasers: Mock sections like "Nominate a Property" (form storing to Replit DB) or "Simulate Phases" (static table with your pricing) to hint at functionality.
Improve Visual Flow: Break long scrolling with section anchors, more icons/images (e.g., vacant properties vs. revitalized), and a closing CTA: "Sign up for notifications on blockchain integration and first pilots."

These low-effort changes (mostly HTML/forms) turn it into an engaging beta landing page, capturing leads for notifications while managing expectations.
